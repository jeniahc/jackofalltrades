Dog Days

by "a Dog Person"

Her name was Razi.

She was a blenheim Cavalier King Charles Spaniel and she was the first pet that our family ever got.

She was a very happy dog with a big and pure heart. A sweet and precious gem. She always found
comfort being near me wherever I went.

And whenever I wasn't around, she would find comfort in her favorite rainbow-colored beanie baby.
She was a special dog. My favorite. And I was her favorite human.

I remember the day she passed, like it was yesterday...

5:00 am She started seizing.
6:00 am I'm at her side, trying to comfort her and help her in any way that I can.
7:00 am We drive to the closest vet that would be able to help with her seizures, which was almost an hour away from our house.
8:00 am The moment we arrive at the vet, right when we park in the driveway, Razi stops seizing. She stops moving completely.
8:15 am Razi is confirmed passed away by the vet. She was 13 years old.

The typical range of death years for her breed are between 9-14 years. So, even though it didn't feel like it,
she did live a pretty full life.

She is survived by her adoptive siblings:

  • Dazzle, a sweet tricolor Cavalier King Charles Spaniel
  • Rafa, a bouisterous Teacup Yorkie
  • & Babyboy, a striped gray cat with the personality of a CEO 

They each have my heart and I love them to death.

But I don't think I'll ever love another pet as much as I loved Razi.
